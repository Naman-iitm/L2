Glossary ‚Äî Common Legal Terms

Quick plain-language definitions of common legal terms that appear in contracts.

Indemnity

A promise to compensate the other party for loss or damage. Plain: If something goes wrong, who pays?

Auto-renewal

Contract automatically renews unless cancelled. Plain: You might be charged again unless you cancel.

Force Majeure

Unforeseen events excuse performance (e.g., natural disasters). Plain: Neither side is blamed for things beyond control.

Liability

Legal responsibility for loss or damage. Plain: Who's legally responsible?

Termination Clause

Conditions under which parties can end the contract. Plain: How and when you or the other party can quit.

Confidentiality

Rules for keeping information private. Plain: What you can't tell others.
